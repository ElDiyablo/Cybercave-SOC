About the Cybercave SOC

Our mission, our people, and why we exist.

// 01 — Mission Statement

Our Mission

"The Cybercave Security Operations Center exists to defend the university's digital infrastructure, develop the next generation of cybersecurity professionals, and serve as a model for student-led security operations. We operate with integrity, transparency, and a commitment to continuous learning — protecting our campus while building skills that translate directly to careers in security."

🛡

Defend

Provide real security value to the university by monitoring live network telemetry and responding to confirmed threats faster than automated systems alone.

📚

Develop

Give students hands-on experience with enterprise-grade tools and processes — the kind of experience that can't be replicated in a classroom lab.

🌐

Serve

Share knowledge, open-source tools, and security awareness resources with the broader campus and local community.

// 03 — History

Program History

The Cybercave SOC grew out of the university's CYS 650 course and summer cyber range operations, evolving from a class project into a functioning security program.

2023

Program Founded

The Cybercave cyber range is established under faculty leadership. Initial infrastructure includes the Palo Alto NGFW and basic ELK stack deployment.

2024

CYS 650 Integration

SOC operations become integrated into coursework. Students begin conducting real monitoring shifts, writing tickets, and using OTX for threat intelligence enrichment.

2025

Summer SOC Operations

First dedicated summer SOC operation. Analysts monitor live campus traffic, develop detection rules, and begin building open-source tooling.

2026

Documentation Site Launch

This GitHub Pages site goes live — centralizing SOC documentation, tools, and resources for current and future analysts.

// 04 — Values

How We Operate

Transparency

We document everything — our processes, our tools, and our findings. Good security doesn't hide behind obscurity.

Integrity

We handle sensitive network data responsibly. Analyst access is for security purposes only, and we operate within defined scope at all times.

Continuous Learning

The threat landscape changes constantly. We stay current through training, threat intel, and honest after-action reviews.

Collaboration

Security is a team sport. We share knowledge openly — within the SOC, with university IT, and with the broader security community.

Student First

This program exists to develop people. Every shift, every ticket, and every investigation is a learning opportunity.

Community Impact

Our long-term goal extends beyond campus — building resources and awareness that help the local community stay safer online.
