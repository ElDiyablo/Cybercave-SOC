// 01 — Mission

Who We Are

The Cybercave Security Operations Center (SOC) is a hands-on, student-operated security program housed within the university's cyber range. Our mission is twofold: to provide real cybersecurity monitoring and incident response for our campus network, and to equip students with the practical skills, frameworks, and tooling used by professional security teams in the field.

Operating under faculty supervision and aligned with industry frameworks like MITRE ATT&CK and NIST, the Cybercave SOC bridges the gap between classroom theory and real-world security work. Analysts here don't just study threats — they monitor live telemetry, triage alerts, write tickets, and practice the full incident response lifecycle inside a controlled but realistic environment.

Whether you're a first-semester student learning what a SIEM is, or an advanced practitioner building detection rules, the Cybercave SOC has a role for you.

🛡

Palo Alto Firewall

Our next-generation firewall is the primary telemetry source. It generates threat logs, traffic logs, and URL filtering events that flow into the ELK pipeline. The PA platform provides app-ID, user-ID, and content-ID visibility that makes our log data rich and actionable.

📦

ELK Stack (SIEM)

Elasticsearch, Logstash, and Kibana form the core of our SIEM. Logstash ingests and normalizes PA logs, Elasticsearch indexes and stores them for fast search, and Kibana provides the analyst-facing dashboard, search interface, and alerting layer. Accessible through the campus cyber range.

📋

Google Forms Ticketing

When an analyst identifies a potential incident or notable event, they submit a structured ticket through our Google Form. This creates a traceable record of the event, the analyst's observations, evidence collected, and recommended next steps — the foundation of professional SOC documentation.

🔭

OTX Threat Intelligence

AlienVault OTX (Open Threat Exchange) provides community-sourced threat intelligence — IP reputation data, malware hashes, domain blocklists, and attack indicators that analysts use to enrich and contextualize alerts seen in the ELK dashboard.

// 03 — Value & Impact

What the Cybercave SOC Solves

🏫

For the University

University networks are high-value targets — thousands of users, open research environments, and sensitive student and institutional data make campuses attractive to threat actors. The Cybercave SOC provides an additional layer of visibility and detection coverage on top of existing IT security, specifically monitoring the threat logs that the Palo Alto firewall generates. We help identify patterns, suspicious activity, and potential policy violations that might otherwise go unnoticed in the daily noise of a busy network.

🎓

For Students

No lab simulation matches the experience of working with real telemetry. SOC analysts at Cybercave gain hands-on experience with enterprise-grade security tools — ELK, Palo Alto, OTX — and practice the full analyst workflow: detecting, investigating, documenting, and escalating incidents. Participants leave with skills, documented experience, and a portfolio of real work directly applicable to SOC analyst, threat intelligence, and incident response roles.

🏘

For the Community

As the Cybercave SOC matures, we aim to extend its reach beyond the campus boundary. Local small businesses, nonprofits, and municipal organizations often have no dedicated security staff and no visibility into their own network threats. Future program goals include community outreach, shared threat intelligence, and security awareness resources that help the broader region defend against the same threats we monitor every day.

⚙️

Building Real Processes

Beyond detection, the SOC builds the operational muscle that makes security programs work: structured ticket writing, escalation procedures, after-action reviews, and documentation discipline. These are the skills that separate experienced analysts from novices — and they can only be learned by doing. Every shift in the Cybercave SOC is a rep toward professional-grade security operations.

New to the SOC? Head to Getting Started to learn what a SOC is, how to get access to the ELK environment, and what your first shift looks like.
