// 01 — Mission

Who We Are

The Cybercave Security Operations Center (SOC) is a hands-on, student-operated security program housed within the university's cyber range. Our mission is twofold: to provide real cybersecurity monitoring and incident response for our campus network, and to equip students with the practical skills, frameworks, and tooling used by professional security teams in the field.

Operating under faculty supervision and aligned with industry frameworks like MITRE ATT&CK and NIST, the Cybercave SOC bridges the gap between classroom theory and real-world security work. Analysts here don't just study threats — they monitor live telemetry, triage alerts, write tickets, and practice the full incident response lifecycle.

// 03 — Services

Service Catalog

What the Cybercave SOC actively provides to the university community.

👁

Continuous Monitoring

24/7 log ingestion and alert monitoring via ELK SIEM. Analysts review Palo Alto threat logs, flagging anomalies and escalating confirmed incidents.

🚨

Incident Response

Structured triage and response workflow for confirmed security events. Tickets are created, evidence documented, and escalation paths followed.

🔍

Threat Hunting

Proactive searches through log data for indicators of compromise not caught by automated rules. Powered by MITRE ATT&CK-mapped hunt hypotheses.

📊

Threat Intelligence

IOC enrichment using OTX, VirusTotal, and AbuseIPDB. Weekly campus threat summaries shared with IT security stakeholders.

🛠

Open Source Tools

Scripts and detection content developed by SOC analysts — shared openly for the community. See the Tools page for what's available.

🎓

Analyst Training

Structured onboarding, shift mentorship, and access to training platforms. Students build real skills on real infrastructure.
