The best way to break into cybersecurity is to do it. We're looking for motivated students who want real experience defending real infrastructure.

📄

Portfolio-Ready Experience

Every shift you work, every ticket you write, and every investigation you run becomes documented, attributable experience you can reference in interviews and on your resume.

🧭

Mentorship

Work alongside experienced analysts and under faculty guidance. Ask questions, make mistakes in a safe environment, and grow your judgment faster than any lab can provide.

🌐

Community & Network

Connect with peers who are serious about security careers. The SOC community extends beyond graduation — alumni, faculty, and industry contacts are part of the network.

// 03 — Apply

How to Apply

Read the Getting Started guide

Familiarize yourself with what the SOC does, what tools we use, and what a shift looks like. The Getting Started page covers everything you need to know before applying.

Reach out to the SOC Lead

Send a message to the SOC Lead (ElDiyablo on GitHub) or speak with your CYS instructor about joining. Include which role you're interested in and a brief note about your background and goals.

Complete onboarding

New analysts complete an onboarding session covering tool access, shift procedures, and the ticketing workflow. Plan for one hour to get fully set up.

Shadow your first shift

Your first shift is a shadow — you observe, ask questions, and get comfortable with the tools. No pressure, no expectations beyond showing up and staying curious.

No experience required for the Analyst Intern role. We train from zero. What matters is that you're motivated, reliable, and willing to learn. Cybersecurity is learned by doing — and this is where you start doing it.

// 04 — Career Paths

Where This Takes You

SOC analyst experience is one of the most direct paths into the security industry. Here's where Cybercave alumni typically go.

🔍 SOC Analyst L1/L2

The most direct path. Cybercave experience maps directly to entry and mid-level analyst roles at MSSPs, enterprise security teams, and government agencies.

🎯 Threat Hunter

Analysts who develop strong detection instincts move into proactive threat hunting — searching for threats that haven't triggered alerts yet.

🛡 Incident Responder

IR roles require the exact skills developed here — triage, documentation, evidence collection, and escalation under pressure.

🔧 Detection Engineer

Building and tuning the detection rules that power SOC operations. A natural progression for analysts who enjoy the technical side of alert logic.

🔐 Penetration Tester

Understanding defender workflows gives red teamers an edge. Many pentesters started their careers on the blue team.

🎓 Graduate School / Research

Documented hands-on security research experience strengthens graduate applications in cybersecurity, computer science, and information assurance programs.
